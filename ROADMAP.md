# ROADMAP.md - Stardew Valley: Living Roots

This document outlines the development roadmap for the "Living Roots" mod, detailing the progression of features from early to late game, based on agroecological principles.

## Core Concept: The Soil Health Bar

All mod mechanics will revolve around "Soil Health," a new property for each tillable tile on the farm (visible when using a hoe or hovering the mouse, ranging from 0% to 100%).

*   **Poor Soil (0-30%):** Slow growth, high chance of pests, lower quality produce (no stars).
*   **Medium Soil (31-70%):** Similar to the base game.
*   **Living Soil (71-100%):** Faster growth, chance of extra harvests, higher quality (silver/gold/iridium), and unlocks new interactions.

**What DESTROYS Soil Health:**

*   Leaving soil tilled and bare (without cover or plants).
*   Monoculture (planting the same crop repeatedly).
*   Using chemical fertilizers (new "Joja Fertilizer" items, which provide an initial boost but cost 5% soil health).

**What BUILDS Soil Health:**

*   Composting and organic fertilizer application.
*   Crop Rotation.
*   Polyculture / Companion Planting.
*   Green Manure.
*   Soil Cover (Mulch).
*   Animal Management.

## Game Progression

### 🎨 Early Game (Year 1 - Learning the Fundamentals)

The focus is on survival and learning the new soil mechanics, being introduced to the basic concepts of agroecology.

*   **New NPC: Elena:** An agronomist and activist who serves as the player's mentor, introducing new mechanics and quests.
*   **Agroecological Welcome Kit:** Replaces the initial parsnip seeds with a kit that includes a Composter and heirloom seeds.
*   **Composting:** Unlocks the Composter (new machine) to transform organic waste into "Compost" (basic fertilizer).
*   **Soil Cover (Mulch):** Ability to craft and apply mulch to prevent weeds and (most importantly) maintain Soil Health.
*   **Simple Polyculture (Companion Planting):** Introduction to "The Three Sisters" concept (Corn, Snap Beans, and Pumpkin) for growth bonuses and soil improvement.
*   **Heirloom Seeds:** Elena starts selling heirloom seeds, which are more sustainable and beneficial for the soil, in contrast to Pierre's hybrid seeds.
*   **Rainwater Harvesting:** New crafting item: Small Cistern, to collect water on rainy days and reduce reliance on external sources.

### 🎯 Mid Game (Year 2-3 - Building the System)

The focus is on integrating systems and creating a self-sustaining cycle on the farm, deepening agroecological practices.

*   **Crop Rotation:** Introduction of a "Rotation Journal" and classification of plants by families (e.g., Brassicas - Cauliflower, Cabbage; Solanaceae - Tomato, Pepper; Legumes - Beans, Peas). Proper rotation offers quality bonuses and improves Soil Health.
*   **Green Manure:** Elena sells "Green Manure" seeds (like clover or oats) to incorporate nutrients into the soil and give a significant boost to Soil Health.
*   **Integrated Animal Management:** Animals left outside in un-fenced areas now eat weeds and insects (Pest Control) and leave manure that increases Soil Health (if mulched) or compacts it (if bare). Introduction of the "Mobile Coop" for rotational grazing.
*   **Beneficial Insects and Beekeeping:** Beehives now have a second function: in addition to honey, they create an aura of "Beneficial Insects." "Insect Hotels" (new crafting) also attract beneficial insects for natural pest control, offering an alternative to pesticides.
*   **Living Pharmacy:** Learn from Elena or Linus to grow medicinal herbs (Rue, Boldo, Chamomile). These can be used to create tonics (energy buffs) or "Natural Sprays" (Pest Control without soil damage).
*   **Bio-construction:** Robin learns from Elena to offer new construction upgrades:
    *   **Adobe Shed:** Cheaper (uses more Clay), keeps artisan goods (cheeses, wines) at ideal temperatures, ripening them faster.
    *   **Green Roof:** House upgrade that allows planting Living Pharmacy herbs on your roof.

### 🏆 End Game (Year 4+ - Sovereignty and Community)

The focus is on expanding the agroecological philosophy throughout the Valley, replacing the Joja system and achieving "Food Sovereignty."

*   **ICLF (Integrated Crop-Livestock-Forestry):** Introduction of new trees (e.g., Acacia, Gliricidia) that fix nitrogen, allowing cultivation between them for drought immunity and faster growth. Animals grazing under these trees are happier and produce "Shaded Milk" or "Soft Wool" (higher quality).
*   **Greywater Recycling:** House upgrade that installs a system to collect water from your sink/shower ("greywater") and store it in tanks, functioning as a passive water source for irrigation (similar to the Well, but fills faster).
*   **Short Supply Chains (The Stardew Fair):** Instead of selling everything in the shipping bin, you can participate in a Weekly Fair (every Saturday) in the town square. Selling directly to residents gives +25% profit and friendship points. However, residents want variety (Polyculture); they won't buy 100 Ancient Fruit Wines. They want 2 Breads, 1 Milk, 3 Tomatoes, 1 Jar of Jelly.
*   **Community Supported Agriculture (CSA):** A new "CSA Box" can be built on the farm. At the beginning of each season, residents (Evelyn, Gus, Clint, etc.) "subscribe" to your CSA. They pay an upfront fee (good for early season). Your obligation: Deliver a varied basket to them every week. Failing to deliver costs friendship. Success greatly increases friendship and (metaphorically) Soil Health.
*   **Collective Kitchen (The New Community Center Quest):** The abandoned JojaMart (if you followed that path) does not become a movie theater. It becomes the "Living Roots Collective Kitchen." This is a town-wide construction project. Once a week (e.g., Sunday evening), a "Kitchen Work Party" event occurs. You and other residents bring ingredients. It's a cooking minigame (like the Luau festival). The result is "Community Meals" (powerful buffs for everyone in town) and an increase in Food Sovereignty.
*   **Food Sovereignty (The Final 'Victory'):** A new metric in your journal. You achieve it when:
    *   Your farm has >80% average Soil Health.
    *   You have at least 10 types of Heirloom Seeds saved.
    *   The Collective Kitchen is operational.
    *   The CSA program has at least 5 families subscribed.
    *   Reward: Pierre stops selling "Joja Hybrid" seeds and only sells locally adapted Heirloom seeds. Morris (from Joja) permanently leaves town. Elena gives you the "Guardian of Living Roots" trophy. The Valley becomes self-sufficient.
